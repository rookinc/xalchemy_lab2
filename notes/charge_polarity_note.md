# Charge Polarity Note
## Working note for the Thalean project

## Purpose
This note records the current next-step hypothesis for how charge-like structure may first appear in the centered particle-family program.

This is not a derivation note.
It is not a Standard Model claim.
It is a structural note.

The goal is to move from:
- family splitting
to
- polarity
as the first honest precursor of charge.

---

## 1. Background

### Centered arena
The centered mode arena is the codimension-one centered subspace obtained after removing the all-ones / Perron mode from Q.

The nontrivial centered families presently come from the nontrivial spectral families of Qhat:
- E18 of dimension 5
- E3 of dimension 4
- E2 of dimension 5

So the current coarse centered family anatomy is:

14 = 5 + 4 + 5

This is the present ordinary centered family geometry.

### Weak refinement
The weak-sensitive operator candidate is:

Qhat_sigma

obtained by pushing the signed lift / cocycle data into the centered sector arena through:

Q_sigma = M S_sigma M^T

and then centering.

The lift/cocycle layer is independent of the ordinary quadratic overlap law, so it is the first natural source of interaction refinement beyond Qhat. 

---

## 2. Shift of question

The old question was:

Which family is the electron?

The better question became:

Which coarse families split under weak refinement?

The next better question is now:

If a family splits, does it split into oppositely behaving subfamilies?

This note treats that opposition as the first structural version of charge.

---

## 3. First polarity notion

Suppose a coarse family E_lambda refines into two subfamilies:

E_lambda -> F_plus ⊕ F_minus

Define the first polarity predicate by:

Pol(F_plus, F_minus) = 1

iff the two subfamilies respond oppositely to the available interaction grammar.

At first, "oppositely" is deliberately broad.
It may mean:
- opposite sign under the weak-sensitive operator
- opposite response to sheet-sensitive lift action
- opposite transformation behavior under a later ordinary interaction operator

The exact implementation is not yet fixed.
The structural role is.

---

## 4. Why polarity comes before charge values

The present architecture is not yet ready to assign numerical charges.

But it is ready to ask whether refined families come in:
- neutral blocks
or
- opposed pairs

This is a much safer first step.

So the current push is:

charge should first emerge as polarity class, not as a scalar number.

That means:
- neutral-like = no polarity pair
- charged-like = belongs to a polarity pair

This is the first honest structural precursor to charge.

---

## 5. Two possible polarities

### weak polarity
Opposition under the signed lift / cocycle layer.

This is the first and strongest current candidate, because the paper already gives a real signed 2-lift with sheet-preserving versus sheet-exchanging behavior. 

### ordinary polarity
Opposition under an ordinary interaction grammar in the centered legible arena.

This is not yet fully constructed.
It belongs to a later stage.

Current rule:
weak polarity should come first.
ordinary polarity can come later.

---

## 6. Family-level charged vs neutral distinction

The first clean split is:

### neutral family
A family or refined subfamily that does not participate in an interaction-opposed pair.

### polarized family
A family that splits into a pair of subfamilies with opposite interaction response.

This gives the first structural charged/neutral distinction without assigning numbers.

---

## 7. Best place to look first

Current strongest suspicion:

The best place to look for polarity is in the two 5-dimensional flank families of the centered anatomy.

Reason:
- the centered arena has coarse shape 5 + 4 + 5
- the two flanks are dimensionally matched
- matched flanks are the most natural place for mirrored or dual refinement
- the 4-dimensional center family still feels like the most natural candidate for a special or neutral backbone block

So the current frontier question is:

Do either of the 5-dimensional flank families split into a polarity pair under weak refinement?

---

## 8. Center-family possibility

A very attractive possible shape is:

- flank families carry polarity
- center family remains neutral or rigid

In that case:
- the 4-dimensional center family would act as a neutral backbone
- the 5-dimensional flanks would host the first charged-like refinements

This is not a claim.
It is the most attractive current structural possibility.

---

## 9. Refined family signature

For a refined subfamily F, the current best working interaction signature is:

Sigma(F) = (W(F), O(F), Free(F), Pol(F))

where:

### W(F)
weak visibility
- 1 if F responds nontrivially to the weak-sensitive operator
- 0 if weak-silent

### O(F)
ordinary visibility
- 1 if F is strongly legible in the ordinary centered geometry
- 0 if ordinary-faint or ordinary-silent

### Free(F)
freedom
- 1 if F survives as a globally legible centered family
- 0 if confined or chamber-local only

### Pol(F)
polarity
- 1 if F belongs to an interaction-opposed pair
- 0 if not

This is not final, but it is the best current refined family signature.

## 10. First rough particle-style reading

### electron-like
(W, O, Free, Pol) = (1, 1, 1, 1)

Interpretation:
- weak-visible
- ordinary-visible
- globally free
- polarity-bearing

### neutrino-like
(W, O, Free, Pol) = (1, 0, 1, 0)

Interpretation:
- weak-visible
- ordinary-faint
- globally free
- not obviously polarity-bearing at this stage

This is only a first sketch.

### quark-like
(W, O, Free, Pol) = (*, *, 0, 1)

Interpretation:
- confined
- polarity-bearing
- not globally free

This is the current most natural confinement-side sketch.

## 11. Best current summary

The first structural version of charge is polarity.

That means:
- not yet a number
- not yet a gauge derivation
- but a split of a coarse centered family into interaction-opposed refined subfamilies

The strongest current test is:

Does weak refinement produce polarity pairs inside one or both 5-dimensional flank families, while leaving the 4-dimensional center family neutral or rigid?

This is the current best frontier.

- that charge has been derived

- that the 4-dimensional family is proven neutral
- that the 5-dimensional flanks are proven charged
- that the Standard Model has been reconstructed

This note records only the current best structural push.
