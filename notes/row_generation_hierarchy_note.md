# Row Generation Hierarchy Note

## Chamber grammar
The 30 theorem columns decompose into chamber blocks:
- B0: 6-role anchor-free body
- B1: 5-role 2-wing
- B2: 5-role 1-wing
- B3: 5-role 0-wing
- B4: 9-role anchored hub

## Hinge skeleton
The three wing hinges are:
- 10 in B1
- 14 in B2
- 18 in B3

These control the row taxonomy.

## Row taxonomy
Rows are classified up to partner sign by:
- hinge subset
- block vector

Singleton hinge subsets support:
- one anchor selector
- one doubled companion species

Two-element hinge subsets support:
- one doubled flank species

## Partner involution
Each non-anchor class splits as:
- R+ = K ∪ S+
- R- = K ∪ S-

where K is the common core.

### Flank law
The partner involution is a global blockwise 2-for-2 swap.

### Companion law
The partner involution is a sparse local retuning outside the dominant wing core.

## Working conclusion
The theorem row system is generated hierarchically by:
1. chamber grammar
2. hinge subset
3. species family
4. partner involution
