# Particle Emergence Note
## Provisional Standard Model dictionary in the Thalean project

## Purpose
This note records the current project-level mapping between parts of the Standard Model and the present Thalean scalar / layer architecture.

This is not a derivation note.
It is not a proof note.
It is a structured exploration note.

The goal is to say where different particle-like objects first become legible in the current Thalean picture.

---

## 1. Background strata

The present Thalean construction appears to have at least three important strata:

### Tier A: substrate
The thalion as G60 transport organism.
This is the chambered local transport body.

Keywords:
- local transport
- chamber structure
- confinement-like behavior
- native organism

### Tier B: mode
The centered quadratic / spherical layer.
This is where stable nontrivial standing-wave-like modes first become legible.

Keywords:
- centered residue
- normalized geometry
- standing wave
- mode identity

### Tier C: interaction
The lift / cocycle / transition layer.
This is where transport between modes, sheet changes, and symmetry-like actions appear.

Keywords:
- sheet exchange
- transition operators
- coupling actions
- mode mediation

## 3. Guiding rule

Not all Standard Model objects should be expected to emerge at the same layer.

The current best working rule is:

- some objects are substrate-local
- some objects are centered modes
- some objects are transition operators
- some objects are global weighting fields

This is important.
The correct ontology here is likely mixed.

That means:
- do not force every particle into a vertex interpretation
- do not force every particle into a scalar interpretation
- do not force every particle into one single layer

## 4. Electron

### current best home
Tier B: centered mode layer

### current reading
The electron most plausibly emerges as a stable centered standing mode.

### reason
A standing wave should not be identified with:
- the raw transport walk
- the trivial all-ones / Perron mode
- the full thalion as such

A standing wave should appear only after:
- quotienting
- centering
- removing trivial drift
- keeping the bounded repeating shape

That is exactly what the centered quadratic / spherical layer appears to do.

### current summary
electron = stable centered standing mode

### current caution
This does not yet identify a specific eigenvector or mode as "the electron."
It only identifies the likely emergence stratum.

## 5. Neutrino

### current best home
Tier B: centered mode layer

### current reading
The neutrino most plausibly emerges as a weakly coupled centered mode.

### reason
Neutrinos do not feel like:
- local chamber bodies
- raw transport binders
- dominant global modes

They feel more like:
- faint standing identities
- weakly coupled or sparse modes
- residual structure with low interaction visibility

### current summary
neutrino = weakly coupled centered mode

### current caution
The present architecture does not yet distinguish mass hierarchy or flavor structure.

## 6. Photon

### current best home
Tier C: interaction layer

### current reading
The photon most plausibly emerges as a neutral symmetry transport between admissible modes.

### reason
Photons feel less like resident standing modes and more like:
- transport of relation
- symmetry-preserving transition
- neutral mediation

That makes them better candidates for operators than for chamber-occupants.

### current summary
photon = neutral transport symmetry

### current caution
No direct gauge derivation is yet present.
This is a role mapping.

## 7. W and Z bosons

### current best home
Tier C: lift / cocycle layer

### current reading
W and Z most plausibly emerge as lift-sensitive or sheet-changing transition operators.

### reason
The signed lift G30 -> G15 distinguishes:
- parallel lift
- crossed lift
- sheet preservation vs sheet exchange

This makes the lift layer the strongest current home for weak-change style objects.

### current summary
W / Z = lift-changing transition operators

### current caution
This is one of the strongest current analogies, but still remains analogical rather than theorem-level.

## 11. Higgs

### current best home
Tier C, possibly partly external / background

### current reading
The Higgs most plausibly emerges as a mode-weighting or mode-selection scalar background.

### reason
Higgs-like behavior feels more like:
- weighting
- selecting
- turning admissible modes into inertial modes

The Standard Model does not currently appear to emerge from one scalar or one graph layer alone.

The present best reading is:

- substrate-like objects emerge near the thalion
- standing-wave identities emerge near the centered quadratic geometry

- weighting-field-like objects emerge as global or background selectors

That means the Standard Model should be read as a multi-layer emergence problem, not as a one-layer lookup table.
