## 3. First family-dynamic notions

For each coarse family E_lambda, define three first dynamic attributes.

### Stability type
Stab(E_lambda) in {rigid, split, mixed}

Interpretation:
- rigid = preserved as a whole under weak refinement
- split = refined into smaller invariant subfamilies
- mixed = entangled with another coarse family under the joint arena

This is the first family-dynamic classifier.

### Polarity type
If a family splits, define:

Pol(E_lambda) in {balanced, polarized}

Interpretation:
- balanced = split into comparably weighted pieces
- polarized = one dominant piece and one weaker residue

This is the first candidate source of visible/faint or charged/neutral-style distinctions.

### Freedom type
For a refined family or subfamily F, define:

Free(F) in {0,1}

Interpretation:
- Free(F)=1 if F survives as a globally legible centered family
- Free(F)=0 if F exists only as confined or chamber-local structure

This is the first bridge to lepton / quark style language.

## 5. Main structural possibilities

### Case A: one flank splits, one flank stays rigid
Then the two 5-dimensional families are not physically equivalent.
This would immediately create a directional asymmetry inside the centered arena.

### Case B: both flanks split in dual ways
Then the flanks may carry a mirrored or complementary taxonomy.
This is one of the richest possible outcomes.

### Case C: the 4-family is the only rigid block
Then the center family is qualitatively special.
This would make E3 a strong candidate for a distinct neutral or backbone family.

### Case D: the center family splits while the flanks stay coarse
This would also be interesting, but currently feels less likely.

### Case E: strong cross-family mixing
Then the coarse 5 + 4 + 5 anatomy is only an ordinary geometry shadow, and the true particle families must be read entirely from the joint arena A.

## 7. Why dynamics matters more than labels

Without family dynamics, statements like:
- electron-like
- neutrino-like
- quark-like

remain mostly metaphoric.

With family dynamics, the project can ask real questions:

- which families split?
- which split asymmetrically?
- which subfamilies remain globally free?
- which collapse into confinement-like local structure?

That is a much stronger architecture.
